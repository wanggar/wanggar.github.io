Should we abolish death penalty?

Should we abolish death penalty?

Written by Gary Wang, Dec.11th, 2019

Today, the United States will execute its final death penalty of the year. The dying inmate is 47 year-old Travis Runnels, who received the sentence for murdering his supervisor at a prison facility 17 years ago. Runnels grew up in a rough neighborhood in Dallas shuttled between his violent, alcoholic parents and attended 10 schools in 9 years. Since his early childhood, he had been both a witness and a victim of domestic abuse, gun violence, and sexual assault. After Runnels arrived on death row in 2005, he began writing letters in his prison cell. He became a voracious reader in many subjects, including self-improvement and writing. As a death row inmate, he published two books about prison and love. Through writing, he started to repent and help others. His words have inspired fellow inmates and provided positive support to their family members. However, such positive growth will come to a halt this evening when the executioner injects a singel dose of lethal chemicals into his body. Within half an hour, a man who has rebuilt his strength and character will be reduced to nothing but a decaying corpse.

21 death row inmates have been executed this year. With no doubt, these offenders deserve legal punishment for the heinous crimes they committed. To uphold the legal justice system, the country should and must punish them. However, punishment exists not just to punish. The ultimate purpose of legal punishment is to bring positive changes to society. It serves to redress the grievance of victims, prevent future crimes, decrease overall prison population, maintain peace and order in society, etc. However, capital punishment is destructive by nature, and this destructive nature is at odds with this constructive ideal. Instead of debating whether or not the death penalty should be abolished, the policy makers could approach the debate with a constructive perspective and ponder whether or not there exist a better alternative that can both punish and transform. In fact, alternatives of this kind, such as life without parole (LWOP) and inmate rehabilitation, do exist and have proven to bring progress in many parts of the world. The death penalty, on the other hand, stagnates the current system and hinders the nation from reaching its social ideal. To maximize its social wellfare, the United States should consider abolishing the death penalty and reallocating social and economic resources spent on capital punishment on early crime prevention and rehabilitation.

For thousands of years, the Confucius followers believed that “people should not impose on others what they do not desire themselves”. This Confucian school of thought is similar to the biblical teaching in Luke 6:31: “Do to others as you would have them do to you.” If humans abhor the act of deliberate killing, then why should we impose death on others? Regardless of the underlying motive, the act of terminating one’s life is intrinsically a form of vice. Seeking justice on behalf of victims is justified, but taking a man’s life in the name of justice is not. According to Amnesty International, the death penalty is the “premeditated and cold-blooded killing of a human being by the state in the name of justice. It violates the right to life. It is the ultimate cruel, inhuman, and degrading punishment.” The proponents may justify the death penalty by arguing that perpertrators violate others’ right to life upon their murder and therefore do not deserve to keep the right of their own. Consequently, murderers should surrender their life to the state and let it decide whether or not they can continue to live. This argument, based on the idea of “Social Contract” by John Locke, implies that as participants of civil society, people should consent tacitly to surrender their life to the authority in exchange for their protection of their own rights to life and freedom. This implied message is morally unsound because when life is included as part of the social binding contract, the protection of life becomes transactional. However, life is a nonnegotiable right and should therefore not even be implicitly included in the social contract in the first place.

In addition, death penalty is cruel and should not be part of a civil society. Currently, lethal injection is the main method of execution in the United States. Despite the fact that it is designed to be as “humane” as possible, the procedure involves gruesome processes. In his or her last hour, once properly dressed, the inmate is escorted to the execution chamber. Then, the inmate is secured to the gurney with lined ankle and wrist restraints. At this time, a cover sheet may be placed over the inmate’s face. After the intimate is fully restrained, the execution team will connect the intimate to an cardiogram machine, which will be monitored for a flat line to determine when the heart stops beating and death occurs. Then, two intravenous (IV) tubes are inserted, one tube in each arm. The intravenous tubes are threaded through an opening in the wall that leads to an exterior room, where the executioner administers doses behind a class window. As soon as the IV tubes are inserted, a saline solution begins flowing into the body. Three chemicals will then be injected, sodium thiopental that induces anesthesia, pancuronium bromide that paralyzes the diaphragm and lungs, and potassium chloride that induces cardiac arrest. Within two minutes after the last dose is administered, a medical technician will announce that the inmate is dead. The total amount of time between when the inmate leaves the preparation cell and when he or she is declared dead may just be only 30 minutes. However, it can take longer when the execution is botched. Complications such as the failure to find reusable veins and drug overdose can prolong the process and cause unbearable suffering. According to Austin Sarat, a professor of jurisprudence and political science at Amherst College, since 1890, 8,776 people were executed and 276 of those executions went wrong. Lethal injection had the highest rate (7.2%) of botched executions. The death penalty, often carried out by the lethal injection method, inevitably exposes inmates to unnecessary torture. It needs to be stopped.

Since the death penalty has inherent flaws, the lawmakers in the United States should entertain adopting LWOP as an alternative. Like death penalty, LWOP provides the benefits of public protection, retribution, and deterence. Undoubtedly, LWOP can protect the public from dangerous offenders. It guarantees that offenders will be permanently incapacited. It readily accomplishes the goal of preventing further crime by the prisoner and consequently restores public faith in the effectiveness of the prison system. Except in the extreme case of an escape, a legal system can almost assure that LWOP will safeguard the public from criminal offenders for the rest of their life - a sanction much more certain than sentencing them to death, given the high success rate of appeal. In a 1992 survey taken by more than 800 jurors, only 40 percent of them supported capital punishment if LWOP was offered as an alternative. Moreover, LWOP has its retributive power. Criminal offenders deserve to be punished. Even if the death penalty is abolished, a substitute punishment with sufficient gravity should be carried out instead. LWOP, referred to as “death by incarceration”, is by no means a clement sentence. It deprives prisoners of liberty, which is a crucial life element, and can lead to chronic moral affliction. It is as severe as the death penalty itself and is therefore a significant retributive punishment. Apart from public protection and retribution, LWOP also satisfies the third objective of the punishment - crime deterrence. Deterrence is in fact seen as a major strength of LWOP. While reviewable life sentences offer little in the way of deterring potential murders, LWOP is undeniably hash and has a deterrent effect that should not be underestimated. In fact, in a study conducted by death penalty researcher J.H. Wright, some LWOP prisoners that he interviewed strongly disapproved their life sentences and would prefer to be executed rathan than kept behind prison bars and deprived of freedom for the rest of their lives. A survey conducted by Wright among death row inmates revealed that LWOP was perceived as harsh of a punishment as death. If LWOP and death are paralleled in terms of their perceived severity, it will be simply erroranous to include that death penalty deters potential murders more than LWOP, or vice versa. Since LWOP and the death penalty are almost indistinguishable in terms of their deterrent effect, it is safe to assume that replacing the death penalty with LWOP will not lead to a drastic increase in homicide-related crimes. In fact, like the death penalty, LWOP sentence meets the criteria of most of the objectives of punishment and offers an alternative to accompolish these objectives without the moral cost of taking more lives.

Apart from being an effective form of punishment, LWOP is also a more economical alternative. While the fiscal cost of providing punishment should not be the main factor determining offenders’ sentence, it is an important long-term issue for policy makers to consider. The estimated average cost of life imprisonment in the United States is approximately one million dollars, according to Death Penalty Infomrtion Center. However, death-penalty cases cost more than life imprisonment. Putting an offender to death involves a long, expensive legal process. Capital cases involve more lawyers, more witnesses, more experts, a longer jury selection process, more pre-trial motions, an entirely separate trial for sentencing, and exclusive court time. Prosecuting a death penalty case from charging to sentencing takes around four years longer than a LWOP case. Additionally, there are potentially decades of appeals. This additional time equals delays in justice for victims, and hard costs for taxpayers. Morover, many death penalty trials are found to be significantly flawed, which leads to 68% of cases being overturned. When prosecutors continue to seek the death penalty after a death sentence has been overturned, taxpayers pay for not just one but multiple capital trials. In a death penalty study conducted by Duke University, the death penalty costs the State of North Carolina $2.16 million more per execution than a non-death penalty murder case with a sentence of life imprisonment. Similarly, the State of Kansas disclosed in its review of death penalty expenses that capital cases were 70% more expensive than comparable non-death penalty cases.

What I learned from my 5,000 Uber rides?

What I learned from my 5,000 Uber rides?

Written by Gary Wang, March 30th, 2018

Imagine your typical Uber experience. You take out your phone, tap on the app, and wait for no more than 5 minutes before you hop in what is usually a prius. Then, for the next ten to fifteen minutes, you are stuck in the car with a stranger and try to avoid awkwardness by engaging in some small talks or not talking at all. Finally, when the trip is over, you hop off and say goodbye to the driver whom you probably won’t ever see again. When you call the Uber next time, you repeat the routine all over again. Hopping in and Hopping off. Just sounds linear as going from point A to point B. But what if I tell you today that there is a chance that we can break this mundane pattern and make our Ubering experience fun, meaningful, and even addictive ? After taking over 5000 Uber rides, yes 5000 Uber rides, in five countries during the past four years, I am here to tell that there is a possibility.

As an international student, I have to use Uber a lot to get around in the States. During high school, I ubered to school in the morning. I ubered back home in the afternoon. I ubered to rehearsal. I ubered to sports practice. I ubered everywhere. Now, it has been four years since I took my first Uber. Now, I meet about 10-12 Uber on average each week. Each time when I am in a Uber, I seize the opportunity to have fun, deep, meaningful conversation with the driver. I even grew so fascinated by many drivers’ life stories and wisdom that I decided to start a blog about Uber drivers.

But, before I became such a Uber addict open to strangers, I was not a rider who was open to conversation with Uber drivers. As a student enrolled in a competitive high school, I was always in the state of hurry. I had a task-driven mind, which only cared about completion and efficiency, not quite so about the process and journey. During my first few Uber rides at the beginning, I saw my backseat as a shelter from stress and social exiciety. I saw it as the space for my complete privacy.

The conversations happened on those 5000 rides made me reflect on my daily conversations with friends, teachers, families. Upon reflection, I found a big difference between what I call “ubersations” and our daily conversations, like the ones that we might’ve had with our professor in the morning, with our parents last night, with potential employers at a recent career fair. The difference is that a Ubersation happens for the sake of just conversation itself while most of our daily conversations happen because of an ulterior motive. In our daily lives, around 30000 words are exchanged between us and others. But, many of our words serve to communicate a goal or purpose. We go visit and talk to a professor during his office hours to get some points back or maybe ask for a rec letter. We talk to our parents because we want to get our spring break plans figured out. We use conversation as a tool or medium to get what we want, to get things done. We do this so often that we start to lose our ability to appreciate conversation itself.

While I do think purposeful conversations are a necessary part of our daily life, they should be balanced out by conversations that are purpose-free. Through those thousands of laidback, carefree conversations that happened on Uber, I discovered conversations’ beauty and intrinsic quality. A ubersation happens when the lives of two complete strangers intersect for a mere 20 minutes in the confined space of a car. It grows as two strangers converse and share with no ulterior motive. It is driven by genuine curiosity and interest in the the life of another human being – making our lives more interesting and exciting. Without curiosity, I would’ve never found out that the driver next me in his modest camry was a billionaire and would’ve missed the lesson he taught me on humility. Without curiosity, I would still be ignorant of the black community living in suburban Cincinnati. Without curiosity, I would never have experienced the music album that allowed me to appreciate an Uber driver’s creativity and artistic dream.

Uber facilitates our past-paced lives, transporting us from point A to point B. But, to some degree, Uber also offers us the opportunity to slow down, by allowing us to take a moment in our hurried lives and refresh our minds with the light of another human being.

Being curious is easy. It is simply a choice between the front and back seat.

优步司机能教我开车吗？

优步司机能教我开车吗？

作者：Gary Wang 日期：2017年10月2日

一直想把我和陌生人司机之间的经历用文字写出来，于是今天算是正式开始了。一个人在美国念高中的时候，和Uber优步司机在一段或长或短的旅程中聊天成为了我最享受并且最受益的一件事。三年，路上一直陪胖着有意思的对话和经历。

有一次在路上，我试着用学了不久的法语和一位刚果难民移民对话，因为他说他思念故土北非的语言和文化.

有一次在路上，我发现了坐在一辆再普通不过雪弗兰驾驶座上的中年白人居然是一位的亿万富翁。那时，少年轻狂遇见了真正的低调和才华。

有一次在路上，一名热爱生命的脑癌症幸存者感叹激励他始终在路上奔波的不是金钱，而生他头上显而易见的手术刀疤。

许多次优步坐车经历过程中冲撞出的思想火花都会让我的心灵得到升华。但这一切美好的经历有一个前提：我得先开口搭讪说话，并且和各种各样的陌生人司机打开心窗地说话。这听起来和父母给我关于陌生人的教育截然不同。

“孩子，不要随便和陌生人搭话的话。”

“因为乘客和司机社会经历，身份，学识上有差距，产生有价值的交流很难。”

我尊重他们的看法，但是我觉得他们可以尝试理解我想表达的一种品质能力---与陌生人或与自己不同的人相处的勇气和艺术。

在美国读12年级的那年，我想学习驾驶。当时， 我只有一张临时驾照。如果想要上路驾驶，我需要一位21岁以上并持有正式驾照的人在副驾驶座监督指导我。 但是，因为美国住家爸爸妈妈平日繁忙，我一时找不到可以教我开车的人。一急之下，灵机一动，叫了一辆优步。对，当时18岁的我真的有了如此天真的想法。我想尝试借一位优步司机的车，同时请他教我如何开车。在等车的时候，我就准备好被拒绝了。想想一个司机怎么会信任一位毫无相识陌生人，怎么会答应把自己车的转让给一个毫无驾驶经验18岁的高中生？ 但是我想试试。来接我的司机叫Paul。 他带着一副墨镜和一顶鸭舌帽。酷酷的外表下隐藏不住他憨厚的微笑。聊了有一段路程后，我们便下车交换了座位。在他的指导下，我们一起在路上一起度过了一个半小时。 一切都比我想的自然许多。

下车后我问Paul：“当你的方向盘在我的手里的时候，你难道一点都不担心吗？

他大致和我说了以下一段话：“你上车不久后，我就很享受我们的对话。感谢你和我分享了你一个人在美国的留学经历和住家生活。你的性格很独特开放。当你问我能否教你开车的时候，我觉得你有点疯狂，因为从来没有人这样问我。但是其实想想还是蛮有意思的。如果下次还想找我学开车，我可以给你我的电话。我信任你。”

一直到现在都没有忘记Paul对我的信任。现在回想这段经历，意识到了这份信任先来源于我对话中表现出对一位陌生人的真诚和从容。

上车没多久，因为他的微笑，我很快地放下了在他面前的拘束并毫无保留地分享了我独自在美国漂泊不定的住家经历。从小在中西部生活长大的他，对一位中国高中留学时的经历产生了同情心及好奇。他从我的口述和神态中渐渐地了解我的真性情，最终才放心把他的方向盘转交给我。

我想真诚友善的搭讪对话是会打破陌生人之间的隔阂，怀疑和不信任，给生活带来意外经历，让生命更精彩有趣的。我想以这种对生活的态度在路上去记录和陌生人司机对话经历，发现更多的意外收获和小喜悦。

拥有和陌生人搭话的勇气 我的生活更美好理想了， 时刻练习和陌生人交流的艺术 我的生活更附有爱了。

上车开门前你我是生人，下车关门后你我是朋友。

Is computer code a language?

Is computer code a language?

Written by Gary Wang, Nov 25th, 2019

The U.S. Congress is considering a bill to allow programming courses to fulfill the foreign language graduation requirement for high school. Cited as the “High School CODES Act”, the bill seeks to prioritize computer science as a key subject in American secondary schools and bolster the next generation of high-tech workers. In a written response published on New York Times, Dr.William Egginton, a professor of the humanities at Johns Hopkins University, argues that programming knowledge cannot replace foreign language education because computer code is not a language. Indeed, computer science and foreign language are two distinct academic subjects, each of which has its own educational values and should be studied independently. However, Egginton failed to acknowledge fundamental similarities between code and human languages. By formal definition, language is the method of human communication, either spoken or written, including the use of words in a conventional, structured, and stylistic way. Under these criteria, computer code, written in a combination of standard English and logic symbols by human programmers, qualifies as a language.

Computer code, just like English, French, or German, has a vocabulary and formal rules that make it work. Modern programming languages such as Python and C++ provide programmers systematic development guidelines. Documented in a usage manual, these guidelines specify how variables should be named, what developers must include as program directives, where methods need to be defined and called, etc. Documentation to programmers is as grammar guide to writers. It describes coding conventions in detail and allows software developers to produce code comprehensible to the compiler. Just as an article with grammatical mistakes can confuse the readers, a program violating these syntax rules can baffle the computer. When a programmer misuses a data type, misplaces an equal sign, adds an extra space, or even omits a semicolon, the fussy compiler will either misinterpret or fail to translate the source code. The level of syntactic rigor and nuance present in computer code is comparable to that of a human language. Even the slightest error can lead to misconstrued ideas.

Apart from syntax rules, computer code also demonstrates a clear use of structures. The building blocks of modern programming languages are 256 ASCII characters. Just as twenty-six English letters can be combined into words, sentences, and paragraphs, those characters can be configured to produce command lines, methods, and classes. In order to bring all the pieces together into a coherent program, programmers have to design a robust software architecture. For example, just behind a single web page, there are three integral parts acting together to ensure a smooth web browsing experience: a base file that displays the web content, a styling file that determines the web appearance, and a script file that controls the web behavior. The content-style-behavior model exemplifies the systematic design behind thousands of lines of computer code. It serves as a design paradigm for programmers to implement highly readable, well-structured software. Other programming concepts such as inheritance and polymorphism can also illustrate the structural design of computer programs. With inheritance and polymorphism, software can arrange objects of the same parent class in a hierarchical order. A nicely ordered object map presents the relationship between different elements in a similar way a book catalogue displays its sections and chapters. Since computer code practices both systematic design and hierarchical order, it successfully meets the structure criterion.

In addition, people with little programming experience might misunderstand computer programs as general monotonous lines of instructions. However, software, just like writing, comes with a variety of levels and styles. Code written by entry-level programmers usually contains redundant statements, unwieldy data structure, and brute-force algorithms. Such code is often inefficient because it takes up a lot of computation power. By contrast, high-quality code follows an economical style. It restricts the program to a few command lines, practices modularity, and uses the best fitting data structure. As a result, the program can execute elegantly with minimal time and space complexity. Moreover, as programmers advance to higher levels and discover more coding frameworks, they will form their individual coding style. Each framework comes with unique coding features. With over one hundred frameworks available for JavaScript alone, developers can now implement the same software in myriad ways rather than conform to one standard solution.

Like human languages, computer code has usage rules, structures, and styles, but what about the ability to tolerate ambiguities and abstractions? As some skeptics might contend, computer code is fundamentally different from human languages because it does not allow ambiguous and abstract ideas the same way natural languages do. Such an argument is simply not true. In fact, as programming languages continue to develop, many of them are able to decide default behaviors for ambiguous source code. For instance, in a Python-scripted program, distinct data types such as “char” and “string” can be determined flexibly by the compiler. The program can cast one type from the other based on users’ input. Compared to low-level coding languages developed in earlier years, high-level ones such as Python, Swift, and JavaScript have introduced much more latitude for programmers to write high-performance code. Coding, therefore, has now become an open-ended process, rather than a rigid task accompanied by stiff punch cards decades ago. Besides its ability to tolerate ambiguities, computer code can also host abstract ideas. With the object-oriented programming(OOP) design paradigm, programmers can now define any object in abstraction and interact with it through functions. Whether it is a physical object such as a car or an abstract concept like speed, computer code can model a virtual instance of any entity and update its state and behavior as program executes. Contrary to what skeptics might believe, program code is just as apt as human languages to process ambiguities and abstractions.

Admittedly, as skeptics might indicate, computer code communicates directly with machines rather than humans. However, this fact does not jeopardize its status as a legitimate language. Human communication can take place either directly between a group of interlocutors or indirectly through a medium. Historically, humans have used a variety of mediums to communicate with each other. From pigeons to drifting bottles, telegrams to cell phones, communication mediums transfer human messages in form of physical text or electronic signals. Likewise, as a piece of software is executed on the circuit, the computer processor reads command lines written by human programmers and then produces corresponding output that appears in front of end users. With the intelligible output generated from the computer, users can repeatedly interact with the program and participate in the human-computer interaction loop. While direct human communication is absent during this two-way process, computer code gradually manifests programmers’ ideas and intentions as users engage with the software. Despite the intermediate processing steps, computer code still originates from a human programmer and eventually reaches a human end user. Therefore, it should by no means be dismissed by skeptics as an invalid form of human communication.

As programming languages evolve, developers will be able to instruct computers in simpler, more intelligible phrases. Computer code, therefore, will resemble human everyday speech even more. While it is unlikely that future generations will adopt coding languages into their daily speech, computer code will be one of the key languages shaping a society more digitized than ever before.

Obama's Bin Laden Speech Analysis

Obama's Bin Laden Speech Analysis

Written by Gary Wang, Oct.28th, 2019

On the night of May 1st, 2011, at a regular MLB baseball match between the Mets and Phillies, the Citizens Bank ballpark stadium witnessed an unprecedented degree of unity among its usually divided spectators. The “U.S.A! U.S.A!” chant erupted among the audience at the start of the 3rd inning and recurred throughout the evening. Fans cheered from the sidelines and embraced one another, celebrating the exhilarating rumor that they had just learned: a team of U.S Navy Seals had just found and killed Osama Bin Laden, the leader of al-Qaeda responsible for the September 11th terrorist attack. Shortly before midnight, the former President Barack Obama corroborated the death of Bin Laden in televised remarks. In what turned out to be his most watched presidential speech, Obama addressed both national and worldwide audience to announce that the United States had brought Bin Laden to justice. The tactfully composed language invigorated American citizens at home and awed foreign communities abroad. Delivered with strength and resolve, the speech transported the country to a state of domestic solidarity and global power.

Following the defeat of a public enemy, the speech aims to galvanize the nation and reinstitute a sense of unity. After a brief opening remark on Bin Laden’s death, Obama uses flashbacks to evoke national collective memory of the attack. He reminds the public of the 9/11 atrocity as he revisits a series of horrific yet indelible images: “hijacked planes cutting through a cloudless September sky; the Twin Towers collapsing to the ground; black smoke billowing up from the Pentagon; the wreckage of Flight 93 in Shanksville, Pennsylvania”. The parallelism structure within the imagery underscores the unrelenting sequence of physical destruction. Though there is no explicit content of human death in Obama’s imagery, the level of civilian casualties perceived from these lines goes beyond what simple, straightforward words could capture. As he continues to describe the emotional toll that the attack incurred on Americans, Obama supplies the audience with images unseen to the world: “The empty seat at the dinner table. Children who were forced to grow up without their mother or their father. Parents who would never know the feeling of their child’s embrace”. In comparison to the first set of imagery, the latter is void of any violent language like “wreckage”, “smoke”, and “hijacked”. However, it still begets regretful reactions from the audience because it translates the loss of victims into poignant everyday experiences. By revisiting the damage and trauma that the 9/11 attack brought to the nation, Obama creates a moment of shared grief, which allows American citizens to identify and connect with each other as they relive their past memory.

With a collective feeling set in background, Obama then identifies two unifying forces that tied American citizens together during the 9/11. He states that Americans were united by their common love for their community and by their courage to retaliate against the enemy; universal love and courage saved the shattered country. According to him, people “offered [their] neighbors a hand and offered the wounded [their] blood in the aftermath of the attack”. As people came together to heal the emotional wounds, they also showed a united “resolve to protect their nation and bring the offender to justice”. By citing the empowering nature of love and courage in his speech, Obama uses these two universal virtues to elevate the American public and lift victims’ families out of the decade-long grief. It is quite common for politicians to employ lofty ideals (compassion and bravery in Obama’s case) to galvanize the public, yet Obama does so more effectively than others because he provides simple, modest truths to validate his language. His truthful depiction of the aftermath shimmers the heart of the American public and makes people think back to the sense of unity that prevailed on 9/11. Due to conflicting party interests, the national solidarity displayed during 9/11 had, at times, frayed. However, it resurfaced on the day of the speech. City residents gathered outside the White House in Washington D.C., celebrating the victorious moment. At the ballpark stadium in Philadelphia, the fans became oblivious to the rivalry since the day marked a victory for every single American. In New York City, volunteers marched down to Lower Manhattan and placed candles at the Memorial of World Trade Center. With 56.6 millions of viewers tuned in simultaneously on 9 national television networks, Obama’s words sensationalize the post 9/11 era and restore public faith in America’s unbreakable character.

Apart from strengthening the bonds between the citizens at home, the speech also solidifies America’s position as a global leader in maintaining world peace and order. After the 9/11, al-Qaeda and its affiliates expanded their network in Europe, Africa, and the Middle East, killing innocents around the world. Over thirty countries and international organizations deemed al-Qaeda as a terrorist group. As Obama reports to the whole world, he proves that the United States, as the backbone of global counter terrorism efforts, is capable of bringing any offender to justice. First of all, he demonstrates the country’s power by enumerating successful anti-terrorism operations in Afghanistan. Then, after he transitions to reveal the bin Laden capture process, he provides ample context to emphasize the complexity of the operation. Phrases like “far from certain”, “many months to run this thread to ground”, and “met repeatedly with the national security team” are packaged in his speech to underscore the substantial effort the nation took to hunt down bin Laden. Finally, when he describes the settlement of the operation, Obama tactfully switches to short, lightweight sentences: “A small team of Americans carried out the operation. No Americans were harmed. They took care to avoid civilian casualties. After a firefight, they killed Osama bin Laden and took custody of his body”. Duality of Madame Roulin

Duality of Madame Roulin

Written by Gary Wang, March.10th, 2018

Across from Claude Monet’s famous artwork Water Lilies at the Saint Louis Art Museum main gallery hangs an oil portrait painting named Madame Roulin, in which the French artist Paul Gauguin depicts a composed, contemplative lady with modest pastoral garments immersed in her own thoughts. As Madame Roulin sits on the armchair, she averts her gaze and looks detached from her surroundings. One of the noteworthy features of the painting that viewers can capture is the position of the human subject relative both to the entire canvas and to the painting’s background. As Madame Roulin’s upper body takes up the entire vertical space fully, with her hair and hands extending beyond the visual frame, she sits off from the center. The artist positions his female figure to the right side of the central vertical axis and leaves the left side partially unoccupied, therefore rendering the painting horizontally asymmetrical and unbalanced. Meanwhile, behind Madame Roulin’s human figure and her armchair surfaces a partial background view of what appears to be another painting made by Gauguin, Blue Trees.i Whether a technique deliberated by the artist or not, the incorporation of a secondary painting within the primary one creates a spatial duality. Relative to the painting Blue Trees, the painted subject is a living human being in real life. Relative to the viewers, Madame Roulin remains a human portrait. Gauguin’s combined use of spatial asymmetry and ambiguity in the painting helps create an ambivalent, complex female character that makes the painting an intriguing piece.

The facial asymmetry in the painting divides Madame Roulin into two distinctive halves, with each representing contrasting personality traits. Madame Roulin’s unaligned eyes contribute significantly to the asymmetry. Under a close examination of the painting, the viewers can notice that Madame Roulin gazes in two different directions. Her left eye looks away from the viewers while her right eye glimpses the center toward them. The facial asymmetry adds complexity to Madame Roulin’s mystical character, which can be manifested if viewers split Madame Roulin’s face in halves and look at them separately. By looking the two halves of Madame’s face one at time, viewers could notice the multitude in her facial complexion. When only the right half of Madame Roulin’s face is considered (left half from the perspective of viewers), she seems to look at the center and establishes a direct eye contact with the viewers who would be standing right in front of the painting. This visual encounter makes the subject an approachable character because such an eye contact can evoke a brief sense of engagement between Madame Roulin and the viewers. However, this fleeting moment of interactivity dissipates as soon as viewers switch their focus to the left side of Madame’s face. The left side of her face appears much more austere and stern. With her left eye staring at an oblique angle, Madame Roulin removes herself from her surrounding and preoccupies with her own vision and thoughts. The detached nature of Madame Roulin’s left-side inevitably creates some distance between the viewers and her. Thus, contrary to Madame’s right cheek, the left cheek generates a feel of indifference and disengagement. Because the two sides of her face possess distinctive and contrasting features, the combination of the two half-faces manifests Madame Roulin into an ambiguous figure of some mysterious and obscure characteristics.

As the human subject’s pronounced contradicting facial features intrigue the viewers at their first sight, the subtle execution of the painting’s background adds a whole new dimension to the large theme of duality and gives rise to an existential paradox. The background painting, Blue Trees, originally features a natural landscape with bold, vivid colors.ii The juxtaposition between a countryside lady and a pastoral painting stresses the human subject’s identification with the land. Whether the painting Blue Trees was behind the human figure at the time of production remains unknown, but the integration of a relevant secondary painting into the primary is quite spontaneous and demonstrates the artist’s self-marketing savvy. Moreover, the background mixes in some interesting twist. Besides the painting Blue Trees, a block of wall intrudes the space and appears at the far-right side in the background. The wall stands behind Madame Roulin but in front of Blue Trees. The human figure, the wall, and the background painting collectively create multiple distinctive layers, thickening the single dimensional plane on a flat canvass. The multitude of layers does not take place in the painting only; the viewers also help create the multitude of the painting. The painting Blue Trees to Madame Roulin is as the painting Madame Roulin to the viewers. To some extent, the artist creates reality within reality. The notion of dual-reality is well illustrated as Madame Roulin undertakes her role as an intermediate being, negotiating between two spatial dimensions and swinging one world to the other.

The treatment of light and shadow in the painting further enhances the spatial ambiguity of Madame Roulin’s existence. Because Madame Roulin sits straight toward the source of light in the painting, Gauguin places a heavy golden yellow color on Madame Roulin’s face. However, Gauguin adds some dark green color in the right side of Madame Roulin’s face and mixes it with the surrounding color to produce shadow. The contrast between light and shadow on her face represents the ambivalence in her character. It is important to note that the shadow and light have their own metaphorical meanings in the painting. Light embodies time, future, and openness whereas shadow induces the feelings of isolation and privacy. The full saturation of sunlight on Madame’s left face enlightens her character and brings her to life. As a result, the left side of her face becomes invigorated, leading to the belief that she is disposed to embrace the exciting, unpredictable future. On the other hand, the shadow on the right side represents a pessimistic side of Madame’s personality; it symbolizes her withdrawal from the liveliness and activity that the other half of her wants to pursue. The shadowed side of her face tells her to stay in the sheltered present.

As the human subject’s pronounced contradicting facial features intrigue the viewers at their first sight, the subtle execution of the painting’s background adds a whole new dimension to the large theme of duality and gives rise to an existential paradox. The background painting, Blue Trees, originally features a natural landscape with bold, vivid colors.ii The juxtaposition between a countryside lady and a pastoral painting stresses the human subject’s identification with the land. The multitude of layers does not take place in the painting only; the viewers also help create the multitude of the painting. The painting Blue Trees to Madame Roulin is as the painting Madame Roulin to the viewers. To some extent, the artist creates reality within reality. The notion of dual-reality is well illustrated as Madame Roulin undertakes her role as an intermediate being, negotiating between two spatial dimensions and swinging one world to the other.

But what techniques does Gauguin use to create both the present and the future in one painting? Plausibly, Gauguin does so through the distortion of light, which is what governs time. In the painting, the skin tone of Madame’s face and that of her hands are different. Gauguin imbues Madame’s face with a bright golden yellow color but paints her hands pale. Theoretically, however, both face and hands should receive the same color treatment because the golden yellow brush strokes on Madame’s clothes suggest that an even distribution of light is shed on Madame Roulin’s full body. But the difference between Madame’s face and hands in color contradicts the idea of even light distribution and therefore disobeys the natural law of light. Moreover, the twisted depiction of the armchair’s shadow adds more credibility to the speculation that Gauguin tries to distort the natural light. The contour of the shadow does not fully match the shape of the object. But if the speculation is true, what effect does Gauguin wish to achieve through the distortion of light? A plausible answer might be that by distorting the natural light, he can simultaneously warp time because time and light are two interlinked entities. As he violates the natural law that governs light and time, he tries to create both the present and the future at the same time in the painting so that the two split versions of Madame’s character can fit in their corresponding time frame. Therefore, the light and shadow together create the imbalance and split of Madame’s character, puzzling the viewers who attempt to grasp a complete picture of the personality that Gauguin assigns to his subject.

The complexity of Madame Roulin lies in the question of whether she is present with the viewers. Seen from a far distance, Madame’s reserved manner detaches her from the viewers. She basks in her own private thoughts, and the image behind her head seems to be her own romantic reverie that features a liberating natural landscape of mountains and trees. She avoids eye contact with the viewers. On a surface level, she looks disengaged and not present with the viewers. However, Gauguin utilizes some other elements of the painting to keep Madame Roulin’s presence. The thick, highlighted black line outlining Madame’s shoulders has a restricting effect on her body. Additionally, Gauguin anchors Madame’s body to the armchair. Because of its firm structure, the armchair can take on the meanings of stability and constancy. It supports and holds Madame’s body, preventing her from leaving the steady present moment. Though Madame loses herself in her own imagination of future, Gauguin does not allow any trace of modernity on her unadorned appearance. She does not wear earrings or necklace. The ruddy facial complexion is her only natural “makeup”. Judged from her outward appearance, she is just a traditional, austere lady.

In attempt to provide a comprehensive analysis of Madame’s character and to address the question about her presence, one may suggest that Madame’s physical being is present with the viewers but her intangible spirit is not. But viewers still cannot fully understand Madame’s character portrayed in the painting because no matter how close viewers look at the painting, there always exists some unconquerable distance between the viewer and the object. Viewed from many angles, she remains reticent and possesses a sense of secrecy. She lives in her own private, reclusive world that others have no access to. Kept on the center of west gallery wall, Madame Roulin is surrounded by the works of other European artists. But, it differentiates itself and basks in its own unique status just as Madame Roulin immerses herself in her own world and awaits next intrigued visitor who tries to fathom her unfathomable character.

La mythologie du Tour de France

La mythologie du Tour de France

Écrit par Gary Wang, Octobre 8, 2019

Tout comme le term « tendon d’Achille », la référence mythologique se présente souvent dans le contexte du sport. Dans son essai écrit en 1957 sur le Tour de France, le philosophe Roland Barthes compare le Tour avec un long récit poétique d’aventures héroïques. Tout en expliquant la similarité entre les deux, il développe une vue ambiguë du Tour et soutient que le Tour de France, même si un fait national, possède des caractères mythiques. Il peut être difficile de comprendre cette vue contradictoire, mais la dichotomie que Barthes fait entre réalité et mythe est bien élucidée par l'interprétation du mot « mythe » et par la nature du sport.

Le mot « mythe » prend plusieurs définitions, y compris les phénomènes surnaturels, la personnage imaginaire dont plusieurs traits correspondent à un idéal humain, ou l’ensemble de croyances idéalisées autour d’un événement. Selon la description par Barthes, le Tour de France est un véritable événement sportif qui inclure tous les trois éléments mythiques. Pendant le Tour, la géographie est personnifiée comme un ennemi qui met le coureur au défi de conquérir le terrain hostile et sinueux. En revanche, le coureur est naturalisé. Il « plonge, traverse, vole, et adhère» pour surmonter les étapes « hirsutes, gluantes, incendiées, hérissées». Ces quatre verbes donnent le coureur une qualité semblable à un animal tandis que les quatre adjectifs rendent le terrain un caractère humain. L'échange entre l’homme et la Nature est un phénomène surnaturel et contribue au mythe du Tour. En plus, pour vaincre le terrain, le coureur n’a pas seulement besoin de la forme (une réalité physique), mais aussi du jump (un ordre surnaturel ou une aide de dieux). Mais, quand le coureur gagne sans l’aide du jump, il est « un héros tout humain, qui ne doit rien à la surnature et tire ses victoires de qualités purement terrestres ».

Barthes prend le coureur Bobet comme un cycliste exemplaire qui nie les dieux et vainc la conjoncture seulement avec son pouvoir humain. Il compte sur sa forme face à l’opposition surnaturelle et représente un humain idéal. Ensuite, Barthes continue à développer sa pensée sur l'idée du sacrifice où il trouve l’intersection entre l'idéalisme et le réalisme du Tour. L’acte de sacrifier pour les coéquipiers symbolise une morale chevaleresque et un esprit idéal, mais il est aussi une stratégie pragmatique. Même si le coureur qui sacrifice possède l'idéal héroïque et altruiste, son « intelligence est ici fonctionnelle» parce que la compétition demande la collaboration. Quand le coureur comme Marcel Bidot dessine une stratégie, il n’est plus naturalisé et est réduit à la « personne d’un analyste méticuleux».

Comme le Tour de France, le match de basket de NBA inclure aussi les éléments mythiques et réels. Beaucoup d’équipes, les New Orleans Pelicans ou les Chicago Bulls par exemple, utilisent un animal comme leur nom et célèbrent l’esprit du animal choisi. Ces équipes s'identifient avec le tempérament de leur animal et le vénèrent tout en créant un culte. La mascotte, le logo, et le maillot sont tous dessinés autour du bête. Parfois, les journalistes comparent les joueurs avec les animaux quand ils décrivent leur style et performance dans le reportage. Kevin Durant est souvent comparé avec une mygale tandis qu’on appelait Shaq O' Neal un requin. Dans ce cas, les joueurs sont aussi naturalisés comme les coureurs du Tour de France. Quand ils sont sur le terrain, ils surmontent les défis posés par la gravité, le temps, et l’air. Par contre, ce qui contribue à la réalité du match, ce sont les spectateurs. Le match de basket de NBA est interactif. L’interaction entre les spectateurs et les joueurs a un effet subtil sur le résultat. Mais, cet effet est compréhensible seulement à l’humain. Quand un joueur fait un lancer franc, les spectateurs font de bruit pour le détourner. L’acte de distraire les ennemis est bien justifié par l’envie humaine de gagner la victoire. Quand le joueur manque le lancer, il a dû être psychologiquement affecté et fait donc une erreur humaine. Mais après tout, ce sont le désir et l'erreur qui donnent le sport plus de réalité.

Un homme politique très influent lors de la fin de l’apartheid, Mandela souligne une valeur extrinsèque du sport: En plus d’un jeu, le sport est aussi un moyen de promouvoir la paix et de réconcilier le conflit entre les différents groupes sociaux. Depuis les jeux olympiques antiques, la branche d’olivier, donnée au champion olympique pendant la cérémonie, est un symbole de non seulement la victoire mais aussi la paix. Bien que la nature du sport promeuve la compétition, le sport est en fait capable de pacifier et d’apporter des changements positifs.

Dans le contexte de politique internationale, le sport fonctionne souvent comme une diplomatie, apaisant la tension et augmentant l'amitié entre les pays. Suite à la Guerre de Corée en 1950, la relation entre les États-Unis et la Chine est restée tendue pendant presque vingt ans. Au cours de cette période, le gouvernement américain a interrompu tous les échanges commerciaux et diplomatiques avec son opposant asiatique. La tension a persisté jusqu’au début des années 70. Les deux pays se sont rendus compte du besoin de s’ouvrir à l’autre. Mais surtout, ce qu’a contribué à ce changement de perspective, c’est le sport. Connu sous le nom de « diplomatie du ping-pong », l’échange entre les joueurs américains et leurs homologues chinois en 1971 a entraîné la détente de la relation et la visite du Président Richard Nixon à Pékin en 1972. La réconciliation a démarré symboliquement un jour pendant les championnats du monde de ping-pong en 1971. Après l'entraînement, Glenn Cowan, un joueur américain, avait raté son bus d'équipe. Après l’avoir vu, le joueur chinois Zhuang Zedong l’a invité au bus d'équipe chinoise. Grâce à ce voyage, les deux joueurs, qui représentaient les deux pays opposés, se sont bien connus et ont échangé les souvenirs à la fin. En plus, l’interaction amicale entre les deux a été saisie par les photojournalists japonais. Le reportage s’est répandu dans les media; il a projeté une image d'amitié et de paix au monde entier. Après ce hasard, l’équipe américaine a reçu une invitation du Président Mao, qui espérait que l’invitation pourrait améliorer la relation entre les pays. Effectivement, la diplomatie du ping-pong a bien réussi. Il a relancé les activités économiques entre les deux pays et a normalisé un ancien relation hostile. Un sport essentiel à l'identité chinoise, le tennis de table a permet aux Etats-Unis de connaître plus de la Chine et a fourni une opportunité pour les deux de se réconcilier. Pendant la visite, les joueurs ont participé à un tournoi amical. En termes du sport, le tournoi a donné une priorité à l'amitié plutôt qu’à la compétition. Au niveau politique, le tournoi a aussi symbolisé un idéal diplomatique qui a valorisé plus la coopération que la rivalité. La diplomatie du ping pong a réussi en quelque sorte à guérir les anciens blessures diplomatiques parce qu’il a permet aux deux parties opposées de se détendre avec un jeu léger et réciproque. Pour les deux pays, le tennis de table était une entité partagée et un terrain d’entente. Il les a réunis et a restauré une relation minée.

Après près de cinq décennies, le pouvoir guérisseur de sport se manifeste encore aujourd’hui. Cette année, les jeux olympiques de Pyeongchang étaient l’occasion de fortifier les relations diplomatiques entre les deux Corée. Pendant les jeux olympiques d’hiver, les joueurs nord-coréennes et sud-coréennes ont marché dans une groupe collective à la cérémonie d'ouverture. Ils ont partagé un nouveau drapeau qui a présenté une péninsule coréenne unifiée. En plus, les deux pays ont aussi formé ensemble une équipe de hockey sur glace. Les joueuses nord-coréennes et sud-coréennes ont joué sous le même maillot et la même bannière. Sur glace, les anciens ennemies sont devenus des alliés. L’impasse diplomatique a disparu comme la mise en jeu a démarré. L'unité entre les deux Corées lors des jeux olympiques d’hiver était un geste paisible pour célébrer la dénucléarisation et la démilitarisation de la péninsule coréenne. Mais pourquoi le sport est-il devenu un moyen nécessaire de communiquer ce rapprochement historique? Compare à un pacte officiel, un événement sportif qui symbolise la réconciliation entre les deux pays peut susciter un effet plus profond parce que le sport, comme une entité universel, peut atteindre et marquer un plus grand public international. La paix née du sport est historique et inoubliable. Cela montre une autre dimension du pouvoir guérisseur du sport; le sport est une guérison non seulement efficace mais aussi durable.

Mais malgré sa capacité d’unir le monde, le sport en certaines circonstances intensifie les conflits et crée la division dans nos sociétés. Puisque la compétition est une partie fondamentale du sport, le sport a souvent une connotation combative et militaire. Quand on parle du sport, on parle parfois en langage de guerre et d'agression. Le tir du basket est comparé avec la balle; un but marqué sur le terrain du football est souvent associé au missile. Effectivement, le sport est inextricablement lié à la guerre au cours de l’histoire contemporaine. En plus d'un événement multisport, les jeux olympiques en 1936 étaient aussi une campagne à promouvoir le régime et la suprématie du nazisme. Avec toute l’attention portée au Berlin, Adolf Hitler a montré le monde la force de l’allemagne, notamment à travers la construction d’un nouveau stade, d’un réseau de télévision, et la domination au tableau des médailles. De plus, les jeux de Berlin se sont déroulés une atmosphère de xénophobie et d'antisémitisme. Les joueurs juifs ont été interdits de participer. Dans ce cas, les jeux olympiques en 1936 montrent bien les limitations du sport à « guérir les blessures» de la société. Sous l’influence de l'antagonisme politique, les jeux dégénèrent en un instrument pour poursuivre les arriere-pensees. Quand les jeux violent les idéaux inhérents au sport, le sport perd son pureté et son pouvoir curatif. Par contre, il crée la fracture sociale dans le monde.

Aujourd’hui, la côte violente du sport existe aussi parmi les ardents supporters. Comme le sport continue de gagner plus de popularité et atteigne un statut presque quasi-religieux dans la société, il fait partie sacrée de l'identité collective dans beaucoup de communautés. Tout en suivant une équipe sportive, les supporteurs investissent non seulement l’argent mais aussi l'émotion dans l'équipe. La croyance commune met les supporters ensemble. En espérant que l'équipe rend la pareille à leur soutien, les supporters attendent collectivement un seul résultat ––– la victoire. L’idéologie autour de la victoire définit l’effervescence que les supporters ressentent quand l'équipe gagne, la déception quand elle perd, la colère quand elle est offensée, et la tendance violente quand l'intégrité de l'équipe est blessée. L’occasion d'émeute liée à l'événement sportif est assez nombreuse dans l’histoire récente. Presque chaque sport a vécu le mort et la blessure de ses fans, mais le football, comme le sport le plus populaire du monde, a subi la tragédie la plus marquante liée à une manifestation sportive. Survenu le 29 mai 1985 au stade de Bruxelle, le drame du Heysel a lieu pendant le match entre le Liverpool et la Juventus, faisant 39 morts et plus de 454 blessés. Les supporters des deux équipes au sommet se sont confrontés. Ils ont détruit des grilles de séparation, et un muret s’est effondré sous la pression et le poids de supporters. Au lieu de construire une relation harmonieuse, le football s’est retourné contre dans ce cas ses supporters fervents et passionnés, entraînant l’escalade de la violence et l'animosité entre les uns les autres .

Comme l’une des plus grandes inventions de la civilisation humaine, le sport possède une dimension constructive et aussi un côté parfois destructif. Dans les deux cas, le sport lui-même n'engendre ni paix ni violence directe. Le type d’effet que le sport a sur nos sociétés dépende de notre interprétation et récit du sport. Les valeurs intrinsèques du sport nous façonnent de manière positive depuis l'Antiquité. Il rend l'espèce humaine plus forte et plus adaptée à la dame nature. Il renforce notre santé, esprit et corps. Ce sont plutôt les qualités extrinsèques du sport qui obscurcissent ses conséquences dans nos sociétés. Comme le sport occupe une place plus en plus importante dans l’espace politique et socio-économique aujourd’hui, il faut que nous négociions une équilibre entre sa pureté et sa qualité rattachée. L'intensité de jeu, la forme, la technicité, la coopération et le style de chaque sport peuvent toujours nous rappeler sa beauté. Si on privilégie et se concentre sur l’essence du jeu, la dimension constructive du sport se manifeste naturellement elle-même. S’ils avaient donné la priorité au processus plutôt qu’au résultat, les supporters des équipes rivales auraient pu se respecter et apprécier le jeu au lieu de se dénoncer. Ils auraient admis la force et la sportivité de l’autre. Par conséquent, le football les aurait réunis dans le même stade au lieu de les diviser. Dans la même logique, si les jeux olympiques en 1936 avaient été purement un événement à célébrer la gloire du sport, il y aurait eu une atmosphère inclusive qui accueille les athlètes sur la base du mérite sportif à la place de l’ethnicité. Au milieu de l’enthousiasme énorme du sport aujourd’hui, nous devons continuer notre confiance au sport parce qu’il continuera de nous soulever, nous définir, et nous réunir.

Le football américain peut-il un jour devenir le sport le plus populaire du monde?
Le football américain peut-il devenir le sport le plus populaire?
Écrit par Gary Wang, Novembre 13th, 2018

Grâce à la globalisation de la culture américaine, le football américain commence de gagner la popularité dans le monde entier. Le nouveau logo d’AFI (Football Américain International) présente un ballon composé des drapeaux différents. La signification symbolique de ce logo exprime une vision globale. Bien que le football américain s'accroît outre-mer, il est vraisemblable qu’il surpasse le football et devienne le sport le plus populaire du monde. Au niveau du jeu, de la base de supporters, et de l'opération, le football possède aujourd’hui un statut incontesté et continuera de rester dominant à l’avenir.

La signification culturelle du football américain renforce ce sport à l'intérieur, mais au même temps elle peut aussi freiner son développement à l'étranger. Quand on parle le football américain, on parle non seulement un sport inventé aux Etats-Unis mais aussi un microcosme de la culture. Alternant entre la défense et l’attaque, le jeu promeut les valeurs très célébrées dans la société américaine: la progression face à l’obstacle, la résistance contre l'agression, la défense du territoire, et la protection d’une possession ou droite commune. Ce sont aussi les valeurs manifestées dans plusieurs chapitres d’histoire au pays, par exemple la guerre d’indépendance. En plus, modifiant le mot «football», l’adjectif «américain» implique un sens de propriété et suggère que ce sport appartient aux États-Unis et fait partie essentielle de l'identité nationale. Puisque ce sport est profondément enraciné dans l'esprit des américains, il faut donc avoir un certain niveau de compréhension du pays pour comprendre sa totalité et sa beauté.

Aujourd’hui, le football américain n’est pas bien compris hors des États-Unis, surtout aux pays orientaux. La ligue nationale de football américain(NFL) s’est entrée sur le marché chinois il y a plus d’une décennie, mais elle a toujour du mal à augmenter sa base de supporteurs chinois et à générer des profits. En fait, beaucoup de téléspectateurs chinois ne comprennent pas exactement la technicité du jeu et les règles. Au lieu d’un sport spectaculaire, les chinois considèrent le football américain comme un sport barbare et violent. Les parents chinois hésitent à laisser ses enfants d’essayer ce nouveau sport; à leurs avis, le football américain est une activité portée à la violence et la blessure, contredisant l'idée de délicatesse valorisée dans la culture chinoise. Plutôt que le sport de physicalité et de combat, la plupart des chinois préfèrent le jeu d’adresse. Cela est la raison pour laquelle les sports comme le football, le tennis, le plongeon et le basket ont déjà rencontré un énorme succès en Chine. Contrairement au football américain, le football est un sport plus intuitif, universel, et peu conflictuel. Il ne nécessite ni beaucoup d’explication des règles ni contexte culturel de saisir sa beauté et sa subtilité. En revanche, si on veut appréhender le charme du football américain, il faut comprendre le sport et même la culture.

Outre le défi culturel, le sous-financement du football américain à l'étranger peut aussi limiter la croissance du ce sport dans le monde entier. Puisque le sport est aujourd’hui devenu une industrie commercialisée, le prospect d’un sport dépende dans une grande mesure d’argent. Grâce à sa popularité inégale aux Etats Unis, la NFL est capable de générer beaucoup de revenues des sponsors, les droites de diffusion, et les ventes de billets, permettant les joueurs d’obtenir un grand morceau de l’assiette financière. Bien que le football américain reçoive beaucoup d’attention aux Etats-Unis, ce sport a en fait peu de financement et de publicité à l'étranger. Il est vrai que le Super Bowl, comme l’un des événements plus regardés aux États-Unis, attire environ 105 million de téléspectateurs chaque année. Mais, la plupart de ces téléspectateurs sont les américains. En plus, par rapport au coup du monde qui a attiré 3.4 billion téléspectateurs autour du monde en 2018, le Super Bowl reste un événement national plutôt que international. En effet, le football américain hors des Etats-Unis n’est pas vraiment prometteur . Tout d'abord, les joueurs du football américain sont mal rémunérés en Europe. Selon un joueur en équipe d'Allemagne, les coéquipiers reçoivent en moyenne une maigre allocation hebdomadaire de 250 euros. Même quand l'équipe nationale voyage pour le tournoi international, les joueurs doivent payer de leur propre proche pour y participer. En Suisse, les joueurs du football américain doivent cotiser 800 euros chaque année pour être en équipe. Au même temps, ils sont aussi responsables pour acheter leur propre équipement de protection. Par conséquent, le sous-financement du football américain crée un grand défi aux les joueurs qui veulent s'entraîner plus rigoureusement. D’un autre côté, le football est un sport mondial bien établi qui attire beaucoup de talent. Les grands clubs fournissent l’opportunité plus désirée aux espoirs recrutés. Les joueurs du football ont l'accès aux meilleures installations de formation, signent le contrat plus gros, et jouissent des supporteurs plus passionnés. En fait, par rapport au football américain, le football a déjà bien créé un écosystème commercial dans le monde entier. Cet écosystème rassemble les joueurs, les supporteurs, et les parrains. Ils contribuent à ce système économique tout ensemble et créent un modèle durable et sans pareil.

Le football américain n’est pas seulement un sport, mais aussi un trésor national. Même si le football américain ne peut pas surpasser le statut du football, il mérite toujours d'être l’un des sports qui forment la société contemporaine aux Etats-Unis. Même si le football américain ne réussit pas à décoller outre-mer, on devrait rester toujours fier et considère cela comme une opportunité de conserver l’authenticité d’un sport américain.
